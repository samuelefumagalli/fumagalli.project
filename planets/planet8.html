NEPTUNE

Neptune is the eighth and furthest planet in the Solar System from the Sun. It was discovered in 1846 on the basis of mathematical calculations and predictions of a backward impact on the orbit of Uranus. Neptune was named after the ancient Roman god of the seas. This gas giant planet is composed mainly of hydrogen, helium and traces of methane, which gives it its bright blue coloring. Methane clouds in the upper atmosphere reflect sunlight, which creates a bright blue hue on the visible side of the planet. Violent atmospheric phenomena such as vortices and storms are characteristic of Neptune, making it one of the most dynamic objects in the Solar System. Neptune has icy clouds and strong winds reaching over 2000 kilometres per hour. These winds create powerful violent storms, including the famous "Great Dark Spot" that was spotted by Russell Hertz in 1989. Studying the extreme conditions on Neptune is helping to expand our understanding of planetary physics and astronomy.
